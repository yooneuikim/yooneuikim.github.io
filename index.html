About

Research overview

My research examines international human rights institutions, particularly how mechanisms like the UN Universal Periodic Review shape state compliance and cooperation. I use network analysis to trace patterns of naming and shaming, text analysis to study state rhetoric, and mixed methods to connect these patterns to outcomes such as treaty ratification and policy change.

A second strand of my work looks at comparative politics and economic development in East Asia, including the political economy of Chinese outward investment and the effects of human rights on regional development.

I received my Ph.D. in Political Science from the University of Iowa in 2021, with fields in International Relations and Comparative Politics and a minor in Methodology. I previously taught at Bentley University and am currently an Assistant Professor at Texas Tech University.
